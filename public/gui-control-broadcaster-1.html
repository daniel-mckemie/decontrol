This page sends control voltage signals to the synthesizer I am playing. The elements on the page will appear/disappear at random, and not everyone in the audience has the same view of the same set of elements. Sometimes your change will effect my instrument, sometimes it won't (like if I unplug the corresponding patch cord), and sometimes it will conflict with another audience members movements (in which case you will hear unpredictable behavior).

×

DECONTROL is a work for electronics and audience. The aim of the work is to utilze the livestream concert environment to further engage the audience at home with what it is they are experiencing. The piece provides a corresponding web page to the viewing audience (or not viewing audience) that allows direct control over the soloist's instrument. The page is a series of sliders and buttons that transmit high resolution, 14-bit MIDI data via websockets to my computer. My computer converts this data to control voltage signals which are then routed to points in a modular synthesizer. I do not know what information the audience will transmit, or when these signals will be transmitted. My only control of the situation is scaling the voltages (where applicable) or unplugging the patch cable and breaking the signal for everyone. It could be imagined that in a concert hall environment, the audience is invited to the stage to turn knobs and push buttons as a collective (the piece can also be performed in-person with mobile devices). You may access the page on your computer, phone, or tablet, so long as your browser supports the Web MIDI API (Chrome or Firefox recommended!).
