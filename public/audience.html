This page sends control voltage signals to the synthesizer I am playing. The elements on the page will appear/disappear at random, and not everyone in the audience has the same view of the same set of elements. Your changes will effect my instrument, and sometimes it will conflict with another audience members movements, in which case you will hear more chaotic results.

DECONTROL for live electronics and audience. The roots of this work were born out of a desire to increase engagement of livestream concerts during the COVID-19 pandemic. The piece provides a corresponding web page to the viewing audience, or not viewing audience, that allows direct control over the soloist's instrument. The page is a series of sliders and buttons that transmit high resolution, 14-bit MIDI data via WebSockets to my computer on stage. My computer converts this data to control voltage signals which are then routed to points in a modular synthesizer. The four qualities of the modular system that are controllable are: frequency, harmonics, utility, and a trigger/sample & hold. I do not know what information the audience will transmit, or when these signals will be transmitted. My only control of the situation is scaling the voltages or unplugging the patch cable and breaking the signal altogether. You may access the page on your computer, phone, or tablet, so long as your device runs Google Chrome (sorry, not iOS/Safari compatible).
